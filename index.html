The Fulbright Program

As a founding Fulbrught Grantee for the Lithuania program, I had the opportunity to spend nearly two years living and working in Lithuania as an English Teaching Assistant (ETA) under the supervision of the U.S. Embassy in Lithuania and the U.S. Department of State. Based in Visaginas, I worked with local schools to instruct students and administrators in American English as well as facilitate cultural exchange in the community. To this end, I launched the 1st annual Spelling Bee at our school in affiliation with the Lithuanian National Spelling Bee. In addition to my primary responsibilities, I also organized town events and founded an English choir as less formal methods to help strengthen the relationship between citizens of our countries.

Over the course of my two terms with Fulbright, I developed a presentation about “Finding Treasure” which I gave at several speaking events. Since graduating from the Fulbright Program, I have stayed involved with the organization as an alumna by mentoring new grantees and participating in conferences, including the 2019 Fulbright Conference where I shared a poster version of my “Finding Treasure” talk.
